SFF Funding Rolling Application
General Information
This application form is for ALL ORGANIZATIONS seeking funding from the Survival and Flourishing Fund (SFF) on the basis of philanthropic impact. This is a rolling application, and may be used regardless of whether a grant round is announced or not. This application combines both the Speculation Grant request, as well as the S-Process Grant Round application.
Only submission of this form is required for the S-Process Main Round. Theme Rounds require submission of an additional supplemental application.
Please submit only one application per organization, per round.
Eligible Entities
Non-profit organizations: Located within a nation not considered adversarial to the US. Must already have charity status, or be hosted by an organization with charity status.
Independent researchers: Must have an established fiscal sponsorship before submission. SFF is unable to recommend funding to individuals.
For-profit companies: Located within the US, UK, Canada, or Australia. Must already be incorporated and have a company bank account. Funding may take the form of a non-dilutive grant or an investment, determined on a case-by-case basis. Non-dilutive grants will be accompanied by a letter like this: Award Letter Example for SFC Grant Recipient
Process Overview
Applicants must be awarded a Speculation Grant in order to be considered guaranteed eligible in an SFF S-Process Grant Round. For further information please visit Speculation Grants.
Applicants will be notified if their Speculation Grant request is approved. Speculation Grants are received and approved on a rolling basis, so there is no set timeline for when you may be awarded. However, you will be notified at most 2 weeks after an application deadline if your request has been approved or not, thereby making your application guaranteed eligible for the current S-Process Grant Round. Your request may still be approved after that point, though unlikely, but it would be automatically guaranteed eligible for the following round.
You may need several sittings to collect all of the information you need for this application. You can save progress on the form if you complete it using a Google account.
You may view a preview of all the questions in this form before starting.
When providing links as part of this application, be sure to make sure the link is configured to be viewable-by-anyone; otherwise your application will be disregarded. If there are Google Docs or files hosted on Google Drive which you aren't able to share in a viewable-by-anyone format, you may submit a PDF copy instead.
Note: Since SFF Advisors may choose to enlist anyone in evaluating your application, restricting links in this way may limit the thoroughness of our evaluation. Similarly, since we may share your application with other potentially interested funders, restricting links in this way may limit your opportunities for funding from other sources.
After submission, you will receive an email with your application ID and a link that allows you to make edits to your submission, if you wish to do so.
It is possible to make late submissions to a round in progress, however your submission will not be evaluated in the current round, and will automatically be submitted to the next grant round to be announced.
To the extent that you need funds urgently, that need can be filled by your Speculation Grant request (which you need to complete in any case to receive funding from an S-Process Grant Round). Speculation Grants can be made quickly and settled in the next round.
For the deadline and information about SFF's current (upcoming) round, please visit our website.
Privacy and LLM Policy
Your application may be viewed by SFF's Fund Advisors, our affiliates, or anyone we choose to enlist in evaluating your application, for the present round and for any future rounds. Your application may be shared with other funders if it is determined they might be interested in funding your org or retroactively evaluating your org during other funding decisions. Beyond that, your materials or our evaluations will not be shared further unless you grant permission to do so.
SFF application reviewers may use AI systems (such as those from Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google) to help process my application. SFF takes reasonable steps to prevent application data from being used to train AI models (such as using provider settings that disable AI training), but cannot guarantee this.
Contact
If you need to reach SFF, you can send an email to sff-contact@googlegroups.com.
Thanks for reading, and best of luck with your application!

Matching Pledge Request
As of 2025, we're offering to structure part or all of any grant recommendation we may make as a Matching Pledge. At a high level, for example, if we offer you a Matching Pledge of $30,000 at a rate of 2x, you can claim $2 of matching funds for every $1 you receive from other donors, up to a total of $30,000 of funds for $15,000 of outside donations.
Applicants who opt in to receiving a Matching Pledge become eligible for an additional pool of funding. The Funder determines a fixed portion of the overall funding to be distributed in the form of Matching Pledges, and only organizations that requested a Matching Pledge are eligible for that portion. From the perspective of our Funders, a Matching Pledge has higher leverage than a standard grant: a $100,000 Matching Pledge that attracts another $100,000 of funding to the receiving organization has a greater impact than the same $100,000 distributed as a standard grant.
A Matching Pledge can also be a powerful tool for you to raise more money overall, since prospective donors are often more motivated when they know their gift will be matched.
See https://survivalandflourishing.fund/matching-pledges for more details on our Matching Pledge program.

Proposed matching rate (required for matching requests)
Type: Multiple choice
What matching rate would you like to use?
You should choose this rate by making a tradeoff between the amount of fundraising required and degree of matching leverage:
A rate of 0.25x ($0.25 from the S-Process for each $1 you raise), 0.5x, or 0.75x, means it will be harder to fundraise enough to claim an entire Matching Pledge, but the pledge will have higher leverage. Higher leverage means Recommenders in the S-Process may set your proposal's matching value more highly, and that more total outside funding is attributable to the Matching Pledge
A rate of 1x ($1 from the S-Process for each $1 you raise) is a balanced approach between amount of fundraising required and degree of matching leverage. This could be thought of as the “default” if you don’t have a strong reason to choose a different ratio.
A rate of 4x ($4 from the S-Process for each $1 you raise), 3x, 2x, or 1.5x means it will be easier to fundraise enough to claim an entire Matching Pledge, but the pledge will have lower leverage. Lower leverage means Recommenders in the S-Process may value your proposal's matching funds less highly, and that less total outside funding is attributable to the Matching Pledge.
Options:
  • 0.25x (highest leverage)
  • 0.5x
  • 0.75x
  • 1x ("default")
  • 1.5x
  • 2x
  • 3x
  • 4x (easiest fundraising)

Maximum standard grant amount (required for matching requests) 
Type: Short answer
This is the maximum amount of funding you'd like to receive as a standard (non-matching) grant.
The S-Process assigns standard funds first, then assigns matching funds from a separate pool. Standard funding your organization receives is taken into account when determining the marginal value of matching funds — so standard funds can "crowd out" matching funds if your organization would be in a position to receive either.
By setting a lower maximum standard grant amount, you can elect for more of your recommendation to come in the form of a Matching Pledge. If you set this amount to $0, then your organization would only receive matching funds (which you'd then need to claim by raising outside donations). If you set it very high, you might receive your entire recommendation as a standard grant, with no matching component.

Proposed deadline for raising outside donations (required for matching requests)
Type: Multiple choice
When would you like your matching deadline to be?
You should choose this deadline based on the timeframe during which you want to be raising outside donations.
Having an earlier deadline can provide more urgency for potential donors, while having a later deadline gives you more time to raise your target amount of donations. You may also want to choose a date that lines up with other external deadlines, such as the end of the year.
(Note that for the 2026 S-Process Grant Round, we expect recommendations to be announced throughout the Fall, so the earliest deadline available is December 31st. We expect Theme Round recommendations to be announced in November, so choosing the December 31st deadline would give very little time for raising outside funds)
In future rounds, we may provide different deadline options.
Options:
  • December 31st of the current year (i.e. the year when your Matching Pledge is announced)
  • March 31st of the following year
  • June 30th of the following year
  • September 30th of the following year
