# Academic Presentation Types Reference

Domain reference for research presentation types supported by the /slides command.

## CONFERENCE: Platform Presentation

**Context**: Presenting original research at a scientific conference.

**Audience**: Peer researchers, experts in the field, session moderators.

**Duration**: 15-20 minutes (typically 12-15 min talk + 3-5 min Q&A).

**Format**: Sequential slides projected to a lecture hall or breakout room.

**Key considerations**:
- Strict time limits enforced by session chairs
- Audience may not be deeply familiar with your specific subfield
- Competing with many other talks in parallel sessions
- Single chance to make an impression; clarity is paramount
- Questions come from experts who may challenge methods

**Common venues**: AHA, ASA, AACR, ASCO, ATS, CHEST, AGU, APS, ACS, IEEE conferences.

## SEMINAR: Departmental Presentation

**Context**: Presenting your research program to departmental colleagues.

**Audience**: Faculty, trainees, graduate students from the department.

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes (typically 40-50 min talk + 10-15 min discussion).

**Format**: Detailed presentation covering multiple studies or aims.

**Key considerations**:
- Audience has general domain knowledge but not specific expertise
- Opportunity to present the full arc of your research program
- More time for methodology details and background
- Informal Q&A throughout is sometimes encouraged
- May serve as a job talk or promotion evaluation

**Common contexts**: Departmental seminar series, job talks, K award presentations.

## DEFENSE: Grant or Thesis Defense

**Context**: Defending a grant proposal or thesis before a review committee.

**Audience**: Review committee, study section members, thesis committee.

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes (typically 20-30 min presentation + 30 min Q&A).

**Format**: Structured presentation followed by extensive questioning.

**Key considerations**:
- Committee has read the proposal; presentation supplements the document
- Emphasis on significance, innovation, and feasibility
- Preliminary data is critical for credibility
- Must address potential pitfalls and alternative approaches
- Q&A is the most important part; anticipate challenges
- Budget justification may be questioned

**Common contexts**: NIH study section, institutional review, thesis defense, qualifying exam.

## POSTER: Poster Session

**Context**: Presenting research at a poster session during a conference.

**Audience**: Conference attendees browsing poster halls.

**Duration**: 1-2 hours of availability; individual discussions 3-10 minutes.

**Format**: Single large poster (typically 48x36 inches) with brief verbal presentation.

**Key considerations**:
- Must be readable from 4-6 feet away
- Viewers may stop for only 30 seconds; visual clarity is essential
- Opportunity for one-on-one in-depth discussion
- QR code linking to paper or preprint is valuable
- Poster should stand alone without the presenter

**Common contexts**: Conference poster sessions, research days, student showcases.

## JOURNAL_CLUB: Paper Review

**Context**: Presenting and critically appraising a published paper.

**Audience**: Lab members, journal club participants, trainees.

**Duration**: 15-30 minutes (presentation) + 15-30 minutes (group discussion).

**Format**: Structured review followed by facilitated discussion.

**Key considerations**:
- Objective presentation of the paper first, then critical appraisal
- Separate strengths from limitations
- Prepare discussion questions to engage the group
- Connect findings to your own research when relevant
- Consider methodological rigor and potential biases
- Discuss clinical applicability and generalizability

**Common contexts**: Lab meetings, journal clubs, evidence-based medicine rounds.

## Mode Selection Guide

| If the user needs to... | Recommend |
|------------------------|-----------|
| Present at a conference | CONFERENCE |
| Give a departmental talk | SEMINAR |
| Defend a grant proposal | DEFENSE |
| Present at a poster session | POSTER |
| Lead a journal club discussion | JOURNAL_CLUB |
| Give a job talk | SEMINAR |
| Present to a thesis committee | DEFENSE |
| Present at grand rounds | CONFERENCE or SEMINAR |
