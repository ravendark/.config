# Funder Types

Understanding different funder categories helps tailor proposals to their priorities, evaluation criteria, and application formats.

## Overview

| Category | Primary Funders | Focus Areas | Typical Range |
|----------|-----------------|-------------|---------------|
| AI Safety | Open Phil, SFF, LTFF | Technical research, existential risk | $50K-$2M |
| Tech Startup | SBIR, State Programs | Commercialization, market potential | $50K-$2M |
| Foundation | Private foundations | Mission alignment, community impact | $10K-$500K |
| Academic | NSF, NIH | Peer review, methodology rigor | $100K-$5M |
| Corporate | Tech companies | Strategic alignment, innovation | $25K-$1M |

## AI Safety Funders

### Open Philanthropy
- **Mission**: Reduce global catastrophic risks
- **Priorities**: Technical AI safety, biosecurity, nuclear risk
- **Format**: Letter of inquiry followed by full proposal
- **Key Criteria**: Theory of change, technical rigor, team capability
- **Typical Timeline**: 3-6 months

### Survival and Flourishing Fund (SFF)
- **Mission**: Support existential risk reduction
- **Priorities**: AI alignment, biosecurity, civilizational resilience
- **Format**: Online application, regrants program
- **Key Criteria**: Tractability, neglectedness, scale
- **Typical Timeline**: 2-4 months

### Long-Term Future Fund (LTFF)
- **Mission**: Improve long-term future outcomes
- **Priorities**: AI safety research, career transitions, community building
- **Format**: Online application
- **Key Criteria**: Expected impact, counterfactual value
- **Typical Timeline**: 1-3 months

## Federal Funders

### NSF (National Science Foundation)
- **Programs**: CAREER, STC, PFI, SBIR/STTR
- **Format**: Highly structured, fastlane/research.gov submission
- **Key Criteria**: Intellectual merit, broader impacts
- **Budget Format**: Detailed line items with modular budgets
- **Typical Timeline**: 6-12 months

### NIH (National Institutes of Health)
- **Programs**: R01, R21, R03, SBIR/STTR
- **Format**: SF424 forms, eRA Commons submission
- **Key Criteria**: Significance, innovation, approach, investigators, environment
- **Budget Format**: Modular or detailed based on amount
- **Typical Timeline**: 9-15 months

## Foundation Funders

### Private Foundations
- **Examples**: Ford, MacArthur, Sloan, Simons
- **Format**: Varies widely, often letter of inquiry first
- **Key Criteria**: Mission fit, organizational capacity, sustainability
- **Budget Format**: Generally simpler than federal

### Community Foundations
- **Examples**: Local/regional community foundations
- **Format**: Online portal, often standardized regionally
- **Key Criteria**: Local impact, community benefit
- **Typical Range**: $5K-$50K

## Startup/Commercial Funders

### SBIR/STTR (Federal)
- **Phase I**: Feasibility ($50K-$275K, 6-12 months)
- **Phase II**: Development ($750K-$1.5M, 2 years)
- **Phase III**: Commercialization (varies)
- **Key Criteria**: Technical innovation, commercial potential, team

### State Programs
- **Examples**: State innovation funds, economic development grants
- **Format**: Varies by state
- **Key Criteria**: Job creation, in-state impact

## Best Practices

1. **Research before applying**: Understand funder priorities and past awards
2. **Match scale appropriately**: Request amounts within typical ranges
3. **Use funder terminology**: Align language with stated priorities
4. **Follow format exactly**: Federal funders reject non-compliant proposals
5. **Build relationships**: Attend information sessions, reach out to program officers
